    How it works

    From two photos to a measured 3D model

    No depth sensors, no turntables, no measuring tape. The pipeline runs on any modern phone or laptop browser.

    Step 01

    Capture two photos

    A front and a side photo in fitted clothing — use the guided camera with its countdown timer, or upload from your camera roll. Your height gives the model real-world scale.

    Step 02

    Send securely

    Photos travel over an encrypted connection (TLS) and are deleted the moment processing finishes. They're also downscaled on your device first, so less of your data leaves your phone at all.

    Step 03

    Explore your 3D twin

    In about 10 seconds you get an interactive 3D model you can spin and zoom, annotated with ~20 of the most-used measurements. Via the API, any measurement can be returned from the model.

    FAQ

    Questions people ask before they scan

    What kind of photos work best?

    Easiest way: use the guided camera on this page. Prop your phone upright at roughly hip height (a shelf or a friend works), tap start when you can see yourself head to toe, and the countdown gives you time to get into place — front view first, then it walks you through the side view.

    Fitted clothing — leggings, shorts, a fitted top — gives the most accurate results; baggy clothes hide your outline. Plain backgrounds and even lighting help, but the reconstruction is robust to normal rooms.

    What happens to my photos?

    They're sent over an encrypted connection (TLS), used once to compute your model and measurements, and then deleted. They're also downscaled on your device before upload, so the full-resolution originals never leave your phone. We don't build a profile of you — there are no accounts on this demo.

    Why does my browser ask for camera permission?

    That prompt comes from your browser, not from us — every website that wants to use your camera must ask, and there's no way around it. Nothing is captured until you start the countdown, the preview stays on your device, and your browser remembers your choice for this site so you're normally only asked once. If you'd rather not grant it, uploading photos works just as well.

    How accurate is it?

    The reconstruction is patented and built for real-world photos — no special hardware or calibration objects. Accuracy details and methodology are available on request for teams evaluating the API.

    What measurements do I get?

    This demo returns about 20 of the most commonly used measurements — chest/bust, waist, hips, inseam, arm length, thigh, neck, and more — each shown on your interactive 3D model.

    That set is just the demo default. Because the output is a full 3D body model, the API can return any measurement defined on it: custom landmarks, garment-specific dimensions, circumferences at any height — whatever your product needs.

    Do I need to install anything?

    No. The demo runs entirely in your browser on any modern phone, tablet, or laptop. For production integrations there's a REST API, and the 3D viewer you see here can be embedded in your own app or site.

    Can I track my progress over time?

    This demo has no accounts, so nothing is stored on our side. You can save each scan on your device or download it as a standalone file, then re-scan in a few weeks and compare. Hosted progress tracking — history, deltas, trend charts — is available to API partners building it into their own products.

    I'm evaluating this for my company. What does integration look like?

    One HTTPS endpoint. Your app sends two photos and a height; the API returns the 3D model plus the measurement set you specify — as structured data, or as the embeddable viewer you see on this page. Most teams have a working prototype in a day. Contact us for a technical walkthrough and we'll integrate against your use case live.

    Something didn't work — what should I check?

    Make sure both photos show your full body head-to-toe, your height is entered, and you're on a reasonably recent browser. Very dark photos or heavy occlusion (long coats, other people in frame) can fail reconstruction. If it still fails, try re-taking the photos — and if you're a developer hitting an integration snag, get in touch.
